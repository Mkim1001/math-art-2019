assignment 18: over and over and over and over and over and over

Today we looked at Lsystems and other fractal patterns -- systems that use recursion to self-reproduce.

These patterns are present or at least observable throughout the natural world.

One interesting palce that they're present is in plants.

We went over a couple of different ways to algorithmically generate plants, one a simple recursive branching and the other a more complicated fern-like L system.

See if you can take the noise that we introduced to the branching tree and put it into the L system.

If that's too easy, you can try using L systems to do other things, like generate timing in sound compositions.

Or take a look at Daniel Shiffman's Barnsley Fern video, another L System.

Post whatever you come up with down below.
